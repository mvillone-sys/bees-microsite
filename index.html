01 — What this means for BEES
The vetting problem, solved

On your call today, you described a recurring challenge: AI generates answers, but your engineers can't tell if the source actually exists, let alone if it's worth acting on. On the O'Reilly learning platform, every answer from the Answers tool is cited, sourced, and referenced to a specific location in a verified expert resource. No more sending engineers off to vet what the model gave them.

Platform beats one-off training

You noted that the AI training you attended today was generic—good effort, hard to apply. That's the nature of episodic programs. The O'Reilly learning platform is always on. When an engineer hits a wall at 10pm on a microservices issue, a deployment problem, or an AI integration question, the answer and the framework for thinking about it are already there.

Your engineers already proved it

During the SDLC custom training in October, your engineers had platform access. No one told them. They found it on their own—and 58 of them used it organically, logging 559 learning sessions and exploring 231 distinct titles. Cursor adoption in your org is uneven because there's no always-available resource to support it. This is that resource.

Meets your team where they already are

Your engineers are in cursor. O'Reilly's MCP integration lets them query expert-vetted content without leaving their IDE. You asked about setting a base prompt so answers don't default to AWS or GitHub—that's exactly how the integration works. Scope it to the tools BEES uses and the answers come back filtered to your stack.
